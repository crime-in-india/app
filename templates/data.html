Crime in India

Glossary

Want to look up what some of the crimes mean? Here is a glossary of some of the terms.

Here is a table of the entire crime data for India. You can download it here.

  • Attempt to murder: You are booked for attempt to murder when you try to kill someone and do not succeed.

  • Culpable homicide not amounting to murder: This means your actions have killed someone but you didn't mean to. The most famous case is the Salman Khan case.

  • Robbery: Robbery is a form of theft in which the offender, during the act, causes, or attempts to cause, death or injury to the victim, according to Indian Kanoon.

  • Dacoity: When five or more people commit robbery, it is considered a dacoity.

  • Sexual harassment and molestation: This is a custom category we have created to combine two crimes: Assault with intent to outrage modesty of women, and insult to the modesty of women.

  • Assault with intent to outrage modesty of women: These crimes entail sexual harassment of women. The offenders booked under this section will have stalked women, committed acts of physically harassing women in a sexual way or molesting them (groping, etc).

  • Insult to the modesty of women: Offenders booked under this category will have passed lewd comments, made gestures or other such acts.

  • Arson: Setting fire to property, either public or private.
